# Ensayos — Fase 2: Re-weave for sovereign tools and urgency dimension

**Spec — 2026-04-28. Bilingual (English + castellano rioplatense).**

## 1. Problem

The series of seven essays (`Ensayos/01-presidencia.md` through `07-carta.md`, mirrored in `Ensayos/castellano/`) closed Fase 1 on 2026-04-28 with deliberate decisions to preserve voice and intervene minimally. Two structural gaps remain:

1. **Essay 1 hedges where the rest of the series does not.** Its Section VI proposes a Swiss-style seven-person Federal Council as the headline alternative to the presidency. This is reformist where the series is revolutionary — it substitutes a smaller throne for a larger one rather than dismantling the throne. Essays 3, 4, and 5 already imply the corrected vision; Essay 1 alone holds the hedge.
2. **The urgency of building sovereign civic tools is missing throughout.** The series names the architecture but not the race. The window for sovereign civic infrastructure is open in 2026 and closing; whoever builds the tools that mediate civic life sets the architecture of the next civilization. The series does not yet carry that strategic sobriety.

The corrected vision: **the alternative to a president is national civic infrastructure that allows citizens to perform the executive function themselves — continuously, at scale, through tools designed to aggregate their judgment, surface their priorities, deliberate at every layer, and convert collective intelligence into coordinated action.** The grey man is co-builder and continuous co-governor, not the recipient of a better-designed government.

## 4. Voice-protection discipline

Eight rules — first four for English, second four for castellano — binding for every session:

1. **Three passes per essay (English).** Re-weave with original side-by-side; read-aloud flag for manifesto-tone; read-against-other-essays for voice drift.
2. **Flag-not-revise.** If a rewrite threatens rhythm, the grey-man figure, Beautiful Part / Move / Mirror structure, or the demolition→construction arc, stop and ask before proceeding.
3. **No platform-doc references in prose.** Not "PLANDIG," not "ANDIG," not "SAPI," not "El Mandato Vivo" inside the essay body. Cartografía at the foot does platform-linking.
4. **Analysis doc grows with us.** Each session ends with a delta entry in `00-ANALISIS.md`. Closing block "Cierre de Fase 2" appended when all seven are done.
5. **Castellano sense-pass, not literal.** Working from the new English; existing castellano held as scaffold but rewritten — not transliterated. English syntax broken; Spanish rhythms found. Direct address in vos throughout.
6. **Read-aloud test in castellano.** A line that reads as English-shadow gets rewritten until it reads as Spanish first. The reader who picks up only the Spanish should never feel the English under it.
7. **Cross-version fidelity, not sameness.** Same argumentative and emotional beats; different sentence shapes, different image-counts allowed. When Spanish finds a sharper image, lift it back to English where possible.
8. **Argentine specifics live more naturally in castellano.** The Spanish is at home; the English is a translation outward.

## 5. Acceptance criteria (per essay, both languages)

- Voice consistent with the rest of the series.
- No manifesto-tone lines, no startup-pitch language, no platform-doc references in prose.
- New content is woven in, not bolted on. A reader who never saw the Fase 1 version cannot identify which lines are new.
- Original-analysis flagged issues for that essay (per `00-ANALISIS.md` §VII) addressed or explicitly waived in delta entry.
- Cartografía at the foot updated for any new connection to the platform.
- Beautiful Part and Move sections preserved as structural elements.
- The Argentine Mirror is anchor, not decoration.
- **Castellano parity:** finished, at the same level of polish as the English, before commit.
- **Native-feel test:** rioplatense reader does not hear English under the castellano; English reader does not sense translation from Spanish.
- **Same beats, different rhythms.**

## 8. Conceptual substrate (from PLANDIG, internalized — never named in prose)

The prose rests on PLANDIG's framing without quoting it:

- *Sistema nervioso secuestrado* — the captured nervous system. Identity, payments, deliberation, memory, compute, AI, education, communication: all currently mediated by infrastructure that does not belong to the citizens who use it.
- *No political sovereignty without economic sovereignty; no economic sovereignty without digital sovereignty.* The thesis rendered in literary English and rioplatense.
- *Build superior alternatives, do not ban competitors.* The principle of construction, not prohibition.
- *Cofre Digital* — your key, your vault. Identity carried like a key, not a permission.
- *Federated, open, capture-resistant by protocol design* — not by promise.
- *Citizens contribute compute (malla comunitaria); citizens deliberate (Paneles); accountable specialists execute under sortition + technical representation.*
- *The Ferreyra register* — abstraction always grounded in a person's day. (Used as a register, not as a literal family in essay prose.)

These appear in the essays as figures, images, and arguments — never as named systems.
