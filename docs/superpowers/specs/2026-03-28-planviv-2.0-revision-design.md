# PLANVIV 2.0 — Revision Design Spec

**Date:** 2026-03-28
**Document:** `Iniciativas Estratégicas/PLANVIV_Argentina_ES.md`
**Source material:** `Iniciativas Estratégicas/MASTER_PLAN HOUSING OS.md`, `Iniciativas Estratégicas/PLAN24CN_Argentina_ES.md`

## Context

PLANVIV is the 15th mandate of the BASTA framework — Argentina's national housing plan. The current version has 20 sections built around 5 pilars. This revision makes four fundamental shifts:

1. **Property freedom over punitive regulation.** Remove all taxes on vacant homes, anti-speculation mechanisms, Airbnb restrictions, and forced market interventions. People do what they want with their property. The state competes, not coerces.
2. **Technology as the supply-side solution.** Instead of taxing vacant stock, build faster and cheaper through Housing OS (ecosystem orchestration platform) + local manufacturing hubs. Technology generates the revenue that taxes were supposed to provide.
3. **Living spaces, not housing programs.** Replace the current "barrios mixtos" model with nested community cells (Núcleo → Célula → Barrio) that build communities, not just houses. Proximity to urban centers is non-negotiable.
4. **Two-lane development.** Bastarda builds at-cost for vulnerable populations. Accredited SMEs build market-rate community projects with transparent margins. Both use Housing OS. Both follow Célula design specs.

## Phase 3: Add SME Track (Section 5.6)

### New Section 5.6: Modelo de Dos Carriles — Bastarda + PyMEs Acreditadas

**Track 1 (Social):** Bastarda at-cost for most vulnerable. Zero margin. Subsidized. Unchanged from current model.

**Track 2 (Market / PyME):**
- Accredited SMEs develop community housing projects
- Transparent margin capped at 10-12% (published, audited) — vs. 20-35% traditional developers
- Must meet Bastarda quality standards (thermal insulation, DVH, accessibility, CPTED, green space)
- Must follow Célula de Desarrollo Comunitario design specs (Phase 4)
- Access to: Bastarda supply chain (aggregate purchasing at Bastarda prices), Housing OS coordination platform, Crédito Hipotecario Bastardo for buyers
- Accreditation: demonstrate construction quality, financial transparency, community specs adherence
- Housing OS tracks performance: on-time delivery, quality scores, buyer satisfaction

**Why two lanes:**
- Bastarda alone can't build 500,000+ units. Needs an ecosystem.
- SMEs create an entire industry of responsible small/medium developers
- Competition between tracks keeps everyone honest
- Ideologically consistent: freedom of enterprise within transparency and quality framework

## Phase 4: Transform Community Model — Células de Desarrollo Comunitario

### 4A. Rewrite Section 5.4 → "Células de Desarrollo Comunitario — Espacios de Vida, No Programas de Vivienda"

**Core reframe:** "La Bastarda no construye viviendas. Construye comunidades. La vivienda es el hardware; la comunidad es el software."

**Nested fractal structure:**

**Núcleo (20-30 familias):**
- Shared internal courtyard (European manzana model)
- Mutual aid network — everyone knows everyone by name
- Shared laundry, tool library, informal childcare arrangements
- The Dunbar core group — intimate, organic, self-organizing

**Célula (5-8 Núcleos = 150-250 familias):**
- Self-sufficient for 80% of daily needs
- Commercial ground floor (emprendedores PLANREP)
- Primary school
- Health post (Centro de Vitalidad PLANSAL)
- Maker space / shared workshop (tools, co-working)
- Community garden (PLANISV connection)
- Playground, small plaza
- Each Célula generates its own economic and social life

**Barrio (Multiple Células):**
- Connected to transit, employment centers, larger urban fabric
- Secondary school, cultural center, sports facility
- Larger commercial offerings
- Governance: Consejo Barrial (Section 19)

**Design principles:**
1. **Proximity to work is non-negotiable.** Build where the city already works, not at the periphery. The cost of segregation exceeds the cost of central land.
2. **Optimize space.** Dense but human-scale. Max 6 pisos without elevator, max 10 with. Internal courtyards, not towers.
3. **Infrastructure, not amenities.** Community gardens, co-working, shared workshops, childcare are infrastructure — as essential as plumbing.
4. **Biological development model.** Inspired by PLAN24CN's city model but at the scale of existing urban fabric. Cells grow, divide, specialize — like tissue.
5. **Both tracks build Células.** Bastarda and SMEs follow the same community design specs.

### 4B. Update Section 19 (Gobernanza Barrial)
- Reframe with Núcleo/Célula/Barrio governance layers
- Núcleo level: informal, neighbor-led, no bureaucracy
- Célula level: lightweight coordination (shared spaces, community garden)
- Barrio level: Consejo Barrial (existing model, largely unchanged)
- Connect to Housing OS Community Self-Governance (Innovation 8, Ostrom)
- Add: maker spaces, shared workshops, community childcare co-ops as governed infrastructure

## Phase 5: Write New Pilar 6 — Plataforma Tecnológica de Vivienda (Housing OS)

### New Section 10: "PLATAFORMA TECNOLÓGICA DE VIVIENDA — EL SISTEMA OPERATIVO DE LA VIVIENDA ARGENTINA (PILAR 6)"

Largest single addition (~300 lines). Structure:

### 10.1 — Por Qué un Sistema Operativo de Vivienda
- Housing operates like pre-EUROCONTROL air traffic: isolated actors, no information flow, manual coordination, massive waste
- Housing OS = platform coordination replacing bureaucratic coordination
- The thesis from the Mendoza master plan, adapted to national framing

### 10.2 — Las 12 Innovaciones a Escala Nacional
Each innovation gets concise national treatment (not Mendoza-level detail):

1. **Housing Switch + DHV** — portable housing identity for all Argentines, central routing across all programs
2. **Construction Air Traffic Control** — layered planning horizons at national scale (strategic/tactical/operational/real-time/post-op)
3. **Deferred-Acceptance Matching** — Nobel Prize algorithm replacing random allocation
4. **Behavioral Delinquency Stack** — 8-layer intervention reducing default from 38% to <10%
5. **5-Layer Trust Architecture / ViviendaCover** — guarantees for all ecosystem actors
6. **Housing Operations Center** — AI-powered exception handling at ANVIV
7. **Supply Chain Orchestration** — aggregate purchasing, JIT, regional staging hubs
8. **Community Self-Governance** — Ostrom-based, connects to Células (Section 5.4)
9. **Dynamic Incentive Signals** — real-time behavior alignment across all actors
10. **Event-Sourced Architecture** — complete state reconstruction, radical transparency
11. **Composable Support Packages** — personalized family journeys, not one-size-fits-all
12. **Platform Bootstrap Strategy** — 9-phase deliberate national rollout

### 10.3 — Hubs de Manufactura Local
The hardware complement to the software platform:
- **3D printing factories** — national network, pilot in 5 cities
- **Prefab panel plants** — one per region, leveraging existing industrial capacity
- **CLT/wood frame facilities** — NEA forestry connection to PLANISV
- **Steel frame production** — scale through Bastarda supply chain
- **Local materials processing** — BTC pressing, hempcrete (PLANSUS hemp), recycled-glass insulation
- All hubs designed to be **SME-operable** (Two-Lane Model, Section 5.6)
- Cross-reference with Section 16 (Innovación Constructiva)

### 10.4 — Software para la Industria de la Vivienda
Platform coordination for ALL ecosystem actors:
- Construction companies: bidding, milestone tracking, quality scoring
- Material suppliers: marketplace, price transparency, aggregate purchasing
- Municipalities: permit workflow, zoning, infrastructure coordination
- Banks: pre-qualification routing, credit management, collection orchestration
- Utilities: service connection scheduling, capacity planning
- Urban planners: demand modeling, land use analytics, digital twin
- Regulators: environmental compliance, seismic verification, automated code checking
- Citizens: application, tracking, payment, community participation, title management

### 10.5 — Arquitectura Técnica Nacional
6-layer platform architecture (adapted from Housing OS Master Plan):
1. Foundation (sovereign cloud, BIM, IoT, open data)
2. Identity & Security (DHV, SSO via RENAPER/Mi Argentina, RBAC)
3. Data & Integration (interop bus, event store, GIS)
4. Trust & Reputation (verification, reputation engine, ViviendaCover)
5. Orchestration Engine (switch, ATC, matching, AI agents)
6. User Interfaces (citizen app, government dashboard, actor portals)

### 10.6 — Integración con PLANDIG y Bastarda Digital
Cross-reference PLANDIG for sovereign cloud, blockchain, digital identity infrastructure.
