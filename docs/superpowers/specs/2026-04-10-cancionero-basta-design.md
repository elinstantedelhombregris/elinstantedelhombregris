# Cancionero ¡BASTA! — Design Spec

**Date:** 2026-04-10
**Status:** Draft
**Purpose:** A Claude Code skill that produces fully-specified, production-ready song documents for the ¡BASTA! movement — lyrics, musical arrangement, performance notes, and AI music-tool prompts — engineered from first principles of music theory, psychology, and mass cultural dynamics to create authentic Argentine anthems across every genre.

---

## 1. The Problem

The ¡BASTA! movement needs to reach every Argentine — across age, class, region, and cultural identity. Political speeches reach the converted. Music reaches everyone. But movement music usually fails because it prioritizes message over craft, producing propaganda that sounds like propaganda. The songs this skill creates must be *genuinely great music first* — indistinguishable from the best of each genre — with the movement's truths woven into the genre's native voice so deeply that the listener is moved before they realize they've been awakened.

## 2. The Vision

Songs that will be sung across the country as hymns of glory and joy. Songs that ignite hearts, bring light to minds, and revelation to spirits. Songs people sing out loud — at asados, in canchas, at marchas, in their cars, in the shower. The destiny of Argentina carried inside the music people already love.

## 3. Core Principles — The Science of Anthems

### 3.1 Neuroscience of Musical Emotion (Frisson Engineering)

The skill doesn't write songs that "sound nice." It engineers specific neurological responses:

- **Dopamine architecture:** Music triggers dopamine through prediction and surprise. The skill specifies chord progressions that establish expectation then violate it at precisely the right moment — the deceptive cadence before resolution, the delayed tonic, the chromatic approach note. Each song document annotates these moments as *Punto de Frisson*.
- **The octave leap principle:** Involuntary chills occur when a melody suddenly expands its range at an emotionally loaded word. "Solo le pido a DIOS" — the leap on "Dios" is not inspiration, it's architecture. Every song specifies where the melodic range expands and on which word.
- **Contour-emotion mapping:** Rising melodies = hope, agency, power. Descending melodies = reflection, lament, gravity. The skill annotates melodic contour per section to ensure the melody's shape mirrors the emotional arc.
- **Harmonic tension ratios:** Each genre has its own harmonic language. The skill specifies not just chords but the *ratio* of tension to resolution — tango tolerates sustained dissonance; cumbia demands frequent resolution; folklore moves between modal ambiguity and tonal warmth.

### 3.2 Psychology of Musical Adoption

- **Limbic bypass:** Music enters through the emotional brain before reaching the analytical brain. You feel before you evaluate. A speech says "think this." A song says "feel this" — and by the time you think, you already agree. The skill exploits this: the ¡BASTA! message arrives wrapped in emotion, not argument.
- **Mere exposure installation:** Hearing a phrase 3-5 times creates neurological preference. The estribillo isn't repeated for structure — it's repeated for *installation*. The skill specifies exact repetition counts optimized per genre's conventions.
- **Earworm mechanics (open-loop hooks):** The brain has a pattern-completion compulsion. A well-designed hook creates an unfinished melodic/rhythmic pattern the brain can't stop trying to close. The skill designs hooks with intentional open loops — melodic phrases that resolve only after the listener mentally replays them.
- **Peak-end rule:** People remember the emotional peak and the ending. Every song document identifies the *momento de verdad* (the peak) and designs the ending to leave the feeling resonating — not a fade-out, but a designed emotional afterimage.
- **Oxytocin through collective singing:** Group singing triggers the bonding hormone. When 50,000 people sing the same estribillo, they become chemically bonded to each other and to the idea. The skill designs for collective singability over solo performance.

### 3.3 Mass Psychology of Cultural Transformation

- **Image over argument (Le Bon):** Crowds think in images, not logic. "La tierra se parte" is an image. "Income inequality is problematic" is an argument. Only one gets chanted. Every lyric must paint, not argue.
- **Memetic design (Dawkins):** Songs are self-replicating cultural units. The most successful are *modular* — a single line can be spray-painted on a wall, chanted at a march, hashtagged, tattooed. Each song document identifies the **línea memética** — the one phrase designed to escape the song and live independently.
- **Identity before ideology:** People join movements because "those are my people," not because they read the platform. The anthem creates belonging FIRST. The ¡BASTA! message arrives after the listener already feels "this is mine."
- **Emotional contagion:** In crowds, emotions propagate exponentially. Songs with clear escalation architecture (quiet → tension → explosive release) turn individual feeling into collective catharsis. The skill designs this escalation curve explicitly.

### 3.4 The Sing-Along Threshold

Research shows an anthem must be learnable in 2-3 listens. This is a hard engineering constraint:

- Estribillos use **pentatonic or near-pentatonic melodies** (universally singable)
- **Syllabic rhythm matches speech rhythm** — the melody moves at the pace of natural speech
- **Open vowels on sustained/strong beats** ("a", "o" carry power and are easy to project)
- **Call-and-response structures** where the crowd has the simplest part
- **Repetitive rhythmic patterns** in the vocal line — the body learns the rhythm before the mind learns the words
- Verses can be sophisticated; the estribillo must be **stupidly simple**

## 4. Song Document Structure

Every song produced by this skill is a complete production blueprint. Each document contains:

### 4.1 Identity Block

```
TÍTULO: [Song title]
GÉNERO: [Primary genre / subgenre]
REFERENCIA SONORA: "[Suena como] X meets Y con la profundidad de Z"
  — 2-3 specific Argentine reference tracks that define the target sound
PILAR ¡BASTA!: [Which of the 6 pillars this song channels]
MANDATO: [Which PLAN(s) this song connects to, if applicable]
AUDIENCIA: [Target demographic — age, class, region, cultural context]
CONTEXTO DE ESCUCHA: [Where this song lives — cancha, marcha, fogón, auto, boliche, TikTok]
ARCO EMOCIONAL: [Emotional state at start] → [Emotional state at end]
```

### 4.2 Musical Specification

```
BPM: [Exact BPM — chosen for physiological effect, not convention]
TONALIDAD: [Key — major/minor/modal, with reasoning]
COMPÁS: [Time signature]
DURACIÓN OBJETIVO: [Target duration]
PATRÓN RÍTMICO: [Precise rhythmic pattern description for the genre's base]
  Example: "Bombo legüero en 6/8 con acentos en 1 y 4, guitarra
  en rasguido de chacarera — chasquido en 2 y 5"

PALETA DE INSTRUMENTOS:
  - [Instrument 1]: [Role, technique, register, when it enters/exits]
  - [Instrument 2]: [Role, technique, register, when it enters/exits]
  - ...

MAPA DE ARREGLO:
  [Intro]: [Dynamics, instruments, energy level 1-10, emotional function]
  [Verso 1]: [Dynamics, instruments, energy level, emotional function]
  [Pre-Estribillo]: [Dynamics, instruments, energy level, emotional function]
  [Estribillo]: [Dynamics, instruments, energy level, emotional function]
  [Verso 2]: [...]
  [Puente]: [...]
  [Estribillo Final]: [...]
  [Outro]: [...]

DIRECCIÓN VOCAL:
  Estilo: [Vocal character — e.g., "raspy, street, como Pity Álvarez circa Tercer Arco"]
  Registro: [Vocal range / tessitura]
  Técnica por sección: [Whisper, belt, spoken word, grito, falsete — mapped to structure]
  Coros: [How backing vocals work — unison, harmony, call-and-response, crowd chant]
  Ad-libs: [Specific ad-libs with placement — "dale", "vamo", breath sounds, gritos]

CONTORNO MELÓDICO:
  [Section-by-section melodic direction — ascending/descending/static/arched]
  [Annotated intervals at key emotional moments]
  [Punto de Frisson markers — exact moments designed to trigger chills]

DINÁMICA DE TENSIÓN-RESOLUCIÓN:
  [Tension curve mapped across the song — numerical 1-10 per section]
  [Moment of maximum tension]
  [Catharsis point — where tension breaks into release]
```

A ready-to-paste prompt block optimized for Suno's syntax:

```
PROMPT DE ESTILO (Suno):
  "[Genre tags], [tempo], [mood tags], [instrument tags], [vocal style tags],
   [production quality tags], [cultural/regional tags]"

PROMPT NEGATIVO:
  "[What to exclude — e.g., 'no autotune, no trap hi-hats, no English']"

NOTAS DE TRADUCCIÓN:
  [How to adapt this prompt for other AI music tools — key parameters to preserve]
```

### 4.4 Lyrics

Full lyrics with inline performance annotations:

```
[Intro — guitarra criolla sola, tempo rubato, como hablando]

[Verso 1 — voz íntima, casi hablada, energía 3/10]
Line 1 of lyrics *(susurrado)*
Line 2 of lyrics
Line 3 of lyrics — [melodic ascent on "palabra clave"]
Line 4 of lyrics

[Pre-Estribillo — energía crece a 6/10, entran percusión y bajo]
Line 1
Line 2 — [PIVOTE NOSOTROS: aquí la voz pasa de "yo" a "nosotros"]

[Estribillo — energía 9/10, coro entra en unísono, DISEÑADO PARA CANTAR EN MASA]
LÍNEA DE ESTRIBILLO *(repetir 2x, la segunda vez más fuerte)*
LÍNEA DE ESTRIBILLO
[Punto de Frisson: cambio armónico en "palabra" — salto de 6ta mayor]

...

[Outro — energía desciende a 4/10, queda solo bombo y voz, último verso a capella]
```

**Language register:** Matched to genre. Lunfardo clásico for tango, villero for cumbia villera, regional for folklore, urbano contemporáneo for trap, poético-callejero for rock nacional. Never generic Spanish — always the specific dialect and vocabulary that makes fans of that genre feel "this is ours."

**Message delivery mode:** Matched to genre. Direct for cumbia/punk, metaphorical for tango/rock, emotional-first for pop/indie, satirical for murga, ancestral for folklore/candombe. The truth arrives in the genre's native storytelling mode.

### 4.5 Anatomía del Alma (Soul Anatomy)

```
LA VERDAD: [The truth being revealed — one sentence]
¿POR QUÉ IMPORTA?: [Why this truth matters to this specific audience]
MOMENTO DE DESPERTAR: [Which line, which section hits hardest — and why]
PIVOTE "NOSOTROS": [Exact moment the song shifts from individual to collective]
LÍNEA MEMÉTICA: [The one phrase designed to escape the song — sprayable,
  chantable, hashtagable, quotable. Short. Rhythmic. Imagistic. True.]
IMAGEN CENTRAL: [The dominant image/metaphor — what the listener sees in their mind]
```

### 4.6 Quality Tests

Every song must pass ALL of these:

```
TEST DE AUTENTICIDAD: Si un fan del género escucha esto sin contexto,
  ¿diría "esto es un temazo" antes de notar el mensaje? [Sí/No + justificación]

TEST DE DESNUDEZ (The Stripped Test): Si quitás toda la producción y queda
  solo voz + un instrumento, ¿sigue emocionando? [Sí/No + justificación]

TEST DEL DÍA DESPUÉS: ¿Podés tararear el estribillo 24 horas después de
  escucharlo 2 veces? [Evaluación de memorabilidad del hook]

TEST DE ESCALABILIDAD EN VIVO:
  - Solo (guitarra + voz): [¿Funciona? ¿Cómo?]
  - Fogón (5-10 personas): [¿Funciona? ¿Cómo?]
  - Cancha (miles): [¿Funciona? ¿Cómo?]
  - Marcha (movimiento): [¿Funciona? ¿Cómo?]
  - Plaza (nación): [¿Funciona? ¿Cómo?]

TEST DE CLIP VIRAL: ¿Hay un segmento de 15-30 segundos que funciona
  como clip standalone para TikTok/Reels? [Identificar el segmento exacto]
```

## 5. Genre Intelligence Database

The skill carries deep structural knowledge of every Argentine genre. Not just "what instruments" but the rhythmic DNA, harmonic language, lyrical tradition, and cultural function.

### 5.1 Tango & Milonga

| Attribute | Specification |
|---|---|
| **Subgéneros** | Tango canción, tango electrónico, milonga campera, milonga ciudadana, vals criollo |
| **Compás** | 2/4 (tango), 2/4 binario (milonga), 3/4 (vals) |
| **BPM rango** | 60-72 (tango canción), 72-90 (milonga), 84-96 (vals) |
| **ADN rítmico** | Arrastre del bandoneón, síncopa 3-3-2, marcato en 4, bordoneo de guitarra |
| **Armonía** | Minor keys dominant, chromatic passing tones, diminished chords for tension, VI-II-V-I, Piazzolla-style extended chords for modern |
| **Instrumentos clave** | Bandoneón, guitarra criolla, contrabajo, violín, piano. Electrónico: añadir pads, beats sutiles |
| **Registro lírico** | Lunfardo clásico — "pibe", "mina", "morfar", "afanar", "bulín", "percanta". Narrative, often in past tense. The tango tells a story of loss that becomes wisdom |
| **Modo de mensaje** | Metaphorical, narrative. The truth arrives as a story — a character who lived something, a street that changed, a Buenos Aires that reflects the nation |
| **Referentes** | Gardel, Piazzolla, Pugliese, Goyeneche, Adriana Varela, Bajofondo, Gotan Project |

### 5.3 Rock Nacional

| Attribute | Specification |
|---|---|
| **Subgéneros** | Rock, blues, prog, hard rock, rock barrial, post-punk, new wave argentino |
| **Compás** | 4/4 predominante, 6/8 en baladas, métricas irregulares (prog) |
| **BPM rango** | 70-85 (balada), 100-130 (rock medio), 140-170 (punk-rock) |
| **ADN rítmico** | Batería con groove propio argentino — ni americano ni británico, más "suelto" y sincopado. Riff de guitarra como identidad de canción |
| **Armonía** | Ecléctico — desde I-IV-V hasta progresiones modales (Spinetta), cromatismo (Cerati), power chords (Pappo). El rock argentino no sigue reglas armónicas fijas |
| **Instrumentos clave** | Guitarra eléctrica (limpia y distorsionada), bajo, batería, teclados (Charly), guitarra acústica para intros/puentes |
| **Registro lírico** | Poético-callejero. Puede ser Spinetta (surrealismo lírico), Charly (ironía social), Solari (críptico-profundo), Pity (crudo-existencial). El rock argentino tiene la tradición lírica más rica de Latinoamérica |
| **Modo de mensaje** | Revelación poética. La verdad llega como imagen que estalla — "Canción de Alicia", "Rasguña las Piedras", "Ji Ji Ji". No dice "la sociedad está mal" — muestra un mundo donde sentís que algo está mal |
| **Referentes** | Spinetta, Charly García, Cerati, Pappo, Solari, Calamaro, Fito Páez, Divididos, La Renga, Pity Álvarez |

### 5.4 Cumbia

| Attribute | Specification |
|---|---|
| **Subgéneros** | Cumbia villera, cumbia santafesina, cumbia pop, cumbia digital/electrónica |
| **Compás** | 4/4 (siempre), pero con subdivisiones distintas por subgénero |
| **BPM rango** | 90-105 (villera), 95-110 (santafesina), 100-115 (digital) |
| **ADN rítmico** | Güiro/tumbadora pattern, bajo sincopado marca identidad, teclado con melodía pegajosa, requinto santafesino (guitarra eléctrica con delay) |
| **Armonía** | Simple: I-IV-V-I, I-V-vi-IV. Villera puede ser solo 2 acordes en loop. La armonía no es donde vive la complejidad — es el ritmo y la melodía |
| **Instrumentos clave** | Teclado (protagonista melódico), bajo, güiro, tumbadoras, timbal. Villera: acordeón a veces. Digital: sintetizadores, 808s |
| **Registro lírico** | Villera: villero puro — "gato", "rati", "paco", "barrio", "rancho", "gorra". Santafesina: más romántica, popular. Digital: más universal. Directo, repetitivo, bailable |
| **Modo de mensaje** | Crudo y celebratorio. La cumbia no tiene filtro — dice la verdad de la calle sin metáfora. Pero también celebra: la fiesta como resistencia, el baile como libertad |
| **Referentes** | Damas Gratis, Pibes Chorros, Los Palmeras, Gilda, Mala Fama, Nene Malo, Cumbia Ninja, El Dipy, L-Gante (cumbia 420) |

### 5.5 Cuarteto

| Attribute | Specification |
|---|---|
| **Subgéneros** | Cuarteto cordobés clásico, cuarteto moderno, cuarteto-cumbia fusión |
| **Compás** | 4/4, pero con el patrón rítmico "tunga-tunga" que lo define |
| **BPM rango** | 110-130 |
| **ADN rítmico** | Tunga-tunga: bass drum on 1-3, hi-hat on every 8th, piano/teclado haciendo el patrón sincopado que da el rebote. El groove es todo — si no rebota, no es cuarteto |
| **Armonía** | I-IV-V, I-V-vi-IV. Simple, funcional, al servicio del ritmo y la melodía |
| **Instrumentos clave** | Teclado (protagonista absoluto), bajo, batería (patrón tunga-tunga), guitarra rítmica, trompeta/saxo opcionales |
| **Registro lírico** | Cordobés — "culiado", "dale bo", "qué onda". Fiestero, romántico, humorístico. El cuarteto es alegría pura |
| **Modo de mensaje** | Joy and street wisdom. El cuarteto lleva la verdad dentro de la fiesta — el mensaje entra bailando |
| **Referentes** | La Mona Jiménez, Rodrigo, La Barra, La Konga, Ulises Bueno, Damián Córdoba |

### 5.6 Trap / Rap Argentino

| Attribute | Specification |
|---|---|
| **Subgéneros** | Trap, rap boom bap, freestyle, drill argentino, RKT |
| **Compás** | 4/4, half-time feel (trap), boom bap directo (rap) |
| **BPM rango** | 65-85 (trap half-time, suena 130-170), 85-100 (boom bap), 130-145 (RKT) |
| **ADN rítmico** | 808 sub-bass con glide, hi-hat rolls (trap), snare on 2-4 (boom bap), dembow riddim (RKT). El silencio es instrumento — los huecos rítmicos dan peso |
| **Armonía** | Minimal — loops de 2-4 acordes, a menudo minor, uso de samples melódicos. La armonía está en el flow vocal más que en los instrumentos |
| **Instrumentos clave** | 808, hi-hats programados, pads/synths atmosféricos, piano melancólico, autotune (según artista). Producción electrónica pura |
| **Registro lírico** | Urbano contemporáneo — "flow", "trap", "pegar", "calle", "real", mezcla español-inglés, neologismos constantes. El trap argentino tiene acento propio — no copia el americano ni el español |
| **Modo de mensaje** | Defiance and raw truth. El trap habla desde el "yo hice" y "yo vi". La verdad es autobiográfica — no cuenta lo que pasa, cuenta lo que vivió. Credibilidad es todo |
| **Referentes** | Duki, Bizarrap, Wos, Trueno, Nicki Nicole, YSY A, Neo Pistea, Tiago PZK, L-Gante |

### 5.7 Pop / Indie Argentino

| **Modo de mensaje** | Emotional-first. La verdad llega como sentimiento compartido — "vos también sentís esto". No te dice qué pensar, te dice qué sentir |

### 5.10 Murga

| Attribute | Specification |
|---|---|
| **Subgéneros** | Murga porteña, murga uruguaya (influencia), murga fusión |
| **Compás** | 4/4, con patrones de bombo con platillo específicos |
| **BPM rango** | 100-130 |
| **ADN rítmico** | Bombo con platillo: patrón de marcha con acentos irregulares, silbato, palmas. El ritmo es callejero — nació en el corso, vive en la calle |
| **Armonía** | Simple, al servicio de la melodía coral. Unísono y armonías simples (3ras, 6tas) |
| **Instrumentos clave** | Bombo con platillo, redoblante, silbato, coro (fundamental), guitarra o teclado opcional |
| **Registro lírico** | Popular, humorístico, satírico. La murga se ríe del poder — usa la ironía, la parodia, el absurdo. "Te lo dice cantando para que no te ofendas" |
| **Modo de mensaje** | Satirical, carnivalesque. La murga dice las verdades más duras con una sonrisa — es la tradición más antigua de crítica social cantada en Argentina |
| **Referentes** | Los Amados, Falta y Resto (uruguaya), La Chilinga, murgas de San Telmo, La Boca, Mataderos |

### 5.11 Candombe

| Attribute | Specification |
|---|---|
| **Subgéneros** | Candombe rioplatense, candombe fusión, candombe-rock |
| **Compás** | 4/4, pero con subdivisión ternaria interna (swing afro) |
| **BPM rango** | 95-120 |
| **ADN rítmico** | Llamada de tambores: chico (agudo, repique), repique (medio, improvisación), piano (grave, base). Tres tambores = un organismo rítmico. El clave de candombe es el corazón |
| **Armonía** | Abierta, modal, a menudo pentatónica. Armonía al servicio del ritmo — los tambores son la armonía del candombe |
| **Instrumentos clave** | Tambor chico, tambor repique, tambor piano. Fusión: añadir bajo, guitarra, teclados. Pero los tambores son sagrados |
| **Registro lírico** | Afro-rioplatense. Lenguaje de resistencia ancestral, herencia, orgullo. Mezcla español con herencia africana. Palabras que son ritmo antes que significado |
| **Modo de mensaje** | Ancestral resistance. El candombe lleva 200 años diciendo "existimos, resistimos, celebramos". El mensaje de ¡BASTA! se funde con esa tradición de dignidad |
| **Referentes** | Rubén Rada, Jaime Roos (uruguayo, influencia), La Vela Puerca (fusión), Hugo Fattoruso, Pedro Ferreira |

### 5.12 Reggae / Ska Argentino

| Attribute | Specification |
|---|---|
| **Subgéneros** | Reggae, ska, rocksteady, dub |
| **Compás** | 4/4, offbeat guitar/keys define the groove |
| **BPM rango** | 70-85 (reggae), 90-110 (rocksteady), 120-150 (ska) |
| **ADN rítmico** | One-drop (reggae), offbeat emphasis, bass weight on 1 and 3, guitarra/teclado en contratiempo. Groove relajado pero propulsivo |
| **Armonía** | Mayor para esperanza, menor para consciencia. Progresiones circulares (I-V-vi-IV), cadenas de subdominante |
| **Instrumentos clave** | Guitarra rítmica (offbeat), bajo profundo, batería (one-drop), teclados (organ/clavinet), vientos opcionales (ska) |
| **Registro lírico** | Consciencia + unidad. Mezcla rasta-rioplatense — "roots", "consciencia", "sistema", "pueblo", "tierra". El reggae argentino habla de justicia con groove |
| **Modo de mensaje** | Consciousness and unity. El reggae no grita — hipnotiza. La verdad llega flotando sobre el groove, se instala por repetición y vibra |
| **Referentes** | Los Cafres, Nonpalidece, Dread Mar-I, Todos Tus Muertos (fusión), Resistencia Suburbana, Fidel Nadal |

## 6. Modes of Operation

### 6.1 Single Song Mode

**Trigger:** User specifies genre + theme, or asks for a song about a topic and lets the skill choose the genre.

**Process:**
1. Analyze the requested genre/theme against the Genre Intelligence Database
2. Select the optimal genre (if not specified) based on message-genre fit
3. Select reference tracks that define the target sound
4. Design the emotional arc and catharsis architecture
5. Compose lyrics in the genre's authentic register
6. Engineer the hook using open-loop and sing-along principles
7. Identify the línea memética and momento de verdad
8. Specify full musical arrangement with frisson points
9. Generate the Suno prompt
10. Run all 6 quality tests
11. Output the complete song document

### 6.2 Album Mode

**Trigger:** User requests a set of songs around a theme, mandate, or pillar.

**Process:**
1. Define the album's narrative arc — what emotional journey does the listener take across all songs?
2. Select genres for maximum variety within coherence
3. Ensure no two songs use the same genre unless justified by subgenre difference
4. Design the track sequence for emotional pacing (opener, build, peak, reflection, anthem closer)
5. Cross-reference lyrics to avoid repetition of images, metaphors, or phrases across songs
6. Produce each song document following Single Song Mode process
7. Add an Album Overview document with the narrative arc, genre distribution, and sequencing rationale

### 6.3 Campaign Mode

**Trigger:** User states a message, mandate, or pillar to spread across Argentina, or asks the skill to design a strategic set.

**Process:**

**Phase 1 — Demographic Intelligence:**
Map Argentina's musical audience segments:

|---|---|---|---|---|
| Jóvenes urbanos AMBA | Trap, indie, electrónica | Buenos Aires metro | 15-28 | TikTok, Spotify, boliches |

| Litoral y mesopotámico | Chamamé, cumbia santafesina | Corrientes, Entre Ríos, Misiones | All | Festivales, fogones |
| Rockeros clásicos | Rock nacional | Nacional | 30-55 | Recitales, asados, auto |
| Juventud politizada | Punk, rap, murga | AMBA, La Plata, Rosario | 16-30 | Centros culturales, marchas |
| Público masivo | Pop, cumbia pop | Nacional | All | Radio, TV, streaming |

| Argentina profunda | Folklore, tango | Interior rural | 35+ | Peñas, fogones, radios AM |

**Phase 2 — Strategic Song Selection:**
- Match ¡BASTA! pillars/mandates to the genres and message modes that deliver them most powerfully to each segment
- Identify demographic gaps — which audiences are hardest to reach? Prioritize them
- Design genre fusions that bridge demographics (trap + folklore bridges jóvenes urbanos with Argentina profunda)
- Select 6-12 songs that maximize total coverage

**Phase 3 — Sequence Strategy:**
- Which songs release first for maximum momentum?
- What's the viral seed? (Usually the catchiest, most shareable track — the one with the strongest 15-second TikTok clip)
- What's the anchor? (The deep anthem that defines the movement — "Solo le pido a Dios" equivalente)
- How do songs reference each other? (Shared motifs, callbacks, a musical DNA that connects them)

**Phase 4 — Production:**
- Produce each song following Single Song Mode process
- Add Campaign Overview document with strategic rationale, demographic mapping, sequence plan, and cross-referencing

### 7.3 Message Delivery Calibration

The skill calibrates message intensity per song:

- **Level 1 — Ambient:** The ¡BASTA! values are in the air but never named. A love song that's really about amabilidad radical. A party track that's really about liderazgo distribuido. The listener absorbs without knowing.
- **Level 2 — Resonant:** The themes are present as images and feelings. "La tierra pide agua" could be about PLANAGUA or could be a love song. The awakened listener hears the deeper layer.
- **Level 3 — Overt:** The message is explicit but arrives through the genre's voice. Direct naming of problems and visions. Still music first, but the ¡BASTA! connection is clear.
- **Level 4 — Anthem:** Full movement anthem. Names the movement. Designed to be sung at marches and rallies. The "Venceremos" or "Bella Ciao" of ¡BASTA!

Each campaign should include songs across all 4 levels for maximum penetration.

## 8. Intergenerational Anchoring System

Songs don't exist in a vacuum — they exist in Argentina's musical memory. The skill deliberately creates connections:

- **Melodic echoes:** A chacarera that contains a 4-note phrase reminiscent of Mercedes Sosa. A rock song whose intro recalls "Rasguña las Piedras." Not plagiarism — *ancestral resonance*. The parent recognizes something, the kid discovers it.
- **Lyrical callbacks:** Phrases that echo classics without copying. If Yupanqui wrote "las penas son de nosotros, las vaquitas son ajenas," the skill might write a line that rhymes with or inverts that structure for modern context.
- **Genre fusion bridges:** A trap song that samples a bombo legüero. A cumbia digital with a bandoneón loop. These fusions physically connect audiences who don't normally share music.
- **The "asado test":** Can three generations sing this together? The grandparent recognizes the folklore root, the parent recognizes the rock influence, the kid recognizes the trap production. One song, three entry points.

## 9. Viral Clip Architecture

Every song must have a designed viral segment:

- **Length:** 15-30 seconds (TikTok/Reels/Shorts optimal range)
- **Placement:** Usually the estribillo or pre-estribillo → estribillo transition
- **Requirements:**
  - Emotionally self-contained (makes sense without hearing the full song)
  - Visually suggestible (listeners can imagine/create videos to it)
  - Contains or leads to the línea memética
  - Has clear rhythmic hook that invites physical response (dance, march, clap)
  - Works as audio-only AND as background for video content

The song document specifies the exact timestamps and why that segment was chosen.
